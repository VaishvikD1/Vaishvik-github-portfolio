The Holy Trinity

Three hybrid hypercars that redefined what a supercar could be.

McLaren P1

A twin-turbo V8 paired with an electric motor for 903 hp combined. Built to be the best driver's car on track and road alike, with active aerodynamics borrowed from McLaren's Formula 1 program.

903 hp • 0–60 in 2.8s • 217 mph
375 units • 2013–2015

Search for more on the McLaren P1

Ferrari LaFerrari

Ferrari's flagship hybrid hypercar combined a naturally aspirated V12 with an F1-derived electric motor (HY-KERS) for 963 hp — the most powerful road car Ferrari had ever built at the time.

963 hp • 0–60 in 2.4s • 217 mph
499 units • 2013–2016

Search for more on the LaFerrari

Porsche 918 Spyder

The most road-friendly of the trinity, the 918 paired a race-derived V8 with two electric motors for all-wheel-drive hybrid performance — and could be driven in pure-electric mode for short distances.

887 hp • 0–60 in 2.5s • 214 mph
918 units • 2013–2015
